QHow does the 30-day re-enrollment loop actually work in HubSpot?
⌄
Confirmed by Sichen: use a 30-day duration step at the end of the workflow.

After E5 sends, the workflow includes a 30-day delay action. The contact stays inside the workflow during this delay — they can't be re-enrolled while already in. Sichen's words: "Re-enroll means they have to exit to rejoin, they can't enter the workflow when they already in the workflow."
QWhy a duration step instead of a date property like last_send_date?
⌄

Sichen's words: "No, there're no last_send_date in criteria. Usually we use duration, adding 30 days for them to exit."

The duration step is HubSpot-native, doesn't require a new property, and is simpler to maintain. Going with this approach.
QWhat needs to be configured in HubSpot for this to work?
⌄
Two things on the workflow itself:

1. 30-day Delay (duration) action placed after E5, before the workflow ends.
2. "Allow re-enrollment" toggled on on the enrollment trigger, so contacts who still match the Active List after the workflow ends get re-enrolled into a fresh cycle.

No custom date property, no Active List criteria changes. Worth a test contact through the full cycle during build to verify behavior before production launch.
